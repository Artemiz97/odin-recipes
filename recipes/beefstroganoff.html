Beef Stroganoff

Beef Stroganoff

is a classic Russian dish that features tender strips of beef cooked in a creamy mushroom sauce, typically served over egg noodles or rice. The dish is known for its rich, savory flavor and comforting texture, making it a popular choice for family dinners and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin or tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Cooked egg noodles or rice (for serving)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil or butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced beef and cook until browned, about 3–4 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they are tender and browned.
  5. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir to combine, cooking for another minute.
  6. Gradually pour in the beef broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  7. Bring to a simmer and let it thicken slightly, about 2–3 minutes.
  8. Reduce heat to low, then stir in the sour cream and Worcestershire sauce. Mix well.
  9. Add the cooked beef back into the skillet, stirring to coat with the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Serve over cooked egg noodles or rice, garnished with fresh parsley.
